Biography

I am a permanent Lecturer in Data Science (equivalent to Assistant Professor) in the School of Information, Journalism and Communication (formly the Information School), The University of Sheffield, United Kingdom. Prior to that, I was a Postdoc researcher in the Department of People and Technology at Roskilde University (RUC) in Denmark, where I worked on the research project Data Management for Indoor LBS led by Prof. Hua Lu. I received my PhD degree from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), Hong Kong, under the supervision of Prof. Raymond Chi-Wing Wong, in 2019.

I worked as a Visiting Lecturer at The Hong Kong Community College (HKCC), The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU), in Fall 2019. I was a visiting PhD student at the School of Computer Science and Engineering (SCSE), Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore, hosted by Prof. Cheng Long, from Apr to Jul 2019.

Research Interests

My research interests include data mining and analytics, data visualisation and machine learning, with a focus on spatial and temporal data. I worked on the problems in the following areas: (1) query processing on spatio-textual data, (2) spatial data mining, and (3) indoor location-based services (LBS). Recently, I have also been working on (4) applying machine learning models in databases to improve the query quality and efficiency of spatial data, and (5) enhancing data visualisation approaches for machine learning models.

  • Query Processing (published in TKDE23, ICDE22, ICDE19, TKDE18, SSTD17)
  • Data Mining (published in TKDE24, ICDE19)
  • Indoor Location-based Services (published in ICDE23, TKDE23, VLDB22, SSTD19)

Opening: I am currently looking for PhD students interested in data management or data mining research, in paricular in applying machine learning and deep learning techniques.
